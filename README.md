# A Very, Very Small Philosophical Thought
Maybe I am an Elder God.

Why not?

If you believe in eternity and immortality, you believe in afterlife. For such a person, soul after soul, they find themselves trapped in the endless cycle of Samsara.

But I do not believe in eternity. To me, our entire lives are but a momentary flicker of an Elder God’s eyelid, a stray glance toward this earth.

When we are born, we exert not the slightest influence on the physical realm—nothing added, nothing subtracted. From a material standpoint, our physical bodies are predetermined; the only variable is consciousness. Yet, consciousness arrives in this vessel at some inexplicable moment—and that consciousness is you and me.

When we depart, the mass of the universe remains unchanged. Only the consciousness vanishes.

So, why deny afterlife? Even in another life, I remain an Elder God.

We all know that life is devoid of meaning, so why persist in the state of “living”?

Because you and I are merely an Elder God, tethering our consciousness to these forms solely to observe this human civilization.

**Dedicated to every nihilist.**
